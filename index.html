GST & Tax Compliance
Does my dealership need a separate GST registration for service and parts income?▼
In most cases, a single GSTIN covers all revenue streams — vehicle sales, service labour, spare parts, and accessories — as long as they operate from the same state and legal entity. If your dealership operates in multiple states or under different entities, each requires its own registration. DealerPay assesses your structure and advises on the optimal setup.
How are FSC/WSC reimbursements treated under GST?▼
FSC and WSC reimbursements are taxable supplies under GST. The dealer raises a tax invoice to the manufacturer for services rendered, and GST is applicable. This must be declared in GSTR-1 and paid in GSTR-3B. Failure to correctly report creates GST mismatches and can attract notices. DealerPay ensures all FSC/WSC invoices are correctly accounted for in your returns.
What GST returns does my dealership need to file every month?▼
A typical automobile dealership files:
  • GSTR-1 — outward supply details (monthly or quarterly)
  • GSTR-3B — summary return with tax payment (monthly)
  • GSTR-9 — annual reconciliation return
  • GSTR-9C — if turnover exceeds ₹5 crore
DealerPay handles all filings, with timely submissions to avoid late fees.
We received a GST notice. Can DealerPay help?▼
Yes. GST notices are typically issued for GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B mismatches, ITC reversals, or non-filing penalties. DealerPay analyses the notice, prepares a detailed response, and files it within the prescribed time. We also conduct a root-cause review to prevent recurrence. Ignoring GST notices can escalate to demand orders.
How is TDS handled for vendor payments at the dealership?▼
Dealerships make payments attracting TDS — rent (194I), contractor payments (194C), professional fees (194J), and others. DealerPay tracks each category, deducts TDS at the applicable rate, deposits it by the 7th of the following month, and files quarterly TDS returns (24Q/26Q). Form 16/16A are generated for all deductees.
Payments & Dashboard
How long does it take for a claim to be paid after submission?▼
Payment timelines vary by manufacturer and claim type:
  • FSC claims — 15 to 30 days after validation
  • WSC claims — 20 to 45 days after docket validation
Delays occur when claims are stuck at "Submitted" due to missing dockets or unsigned invoices. DealerPay monitors each stage and escalates if payment is delayed beyond the expected window.
What does "payment efficiency rate" mean and what is a good target?▼
Payment efficiency rate is the percentage of your total submitted invoice value successfully paid within the billing cycle. If you submitted ₹10L and received ₹9.5L, your efficiency is 95%. A rate below 85% typically signals issues with documentation, missed windows, or unsigned invoices. DealerPay targets 95%+ for every dealer, tracked live on your dashboard.
How do I access my DealerPay dashboard?▼
Once you onboard, you receive a personal dashboard link accessible from any browser — no app required. The dashboard updates in real time as claims are processed. Your dedicated manager also sends a weekly WhatsApp summary so you're always informed without needing to log in.
A payment was received but the amount is less than expected. What should I do?▼
Short payments can occur due to partial claim approval, deductions for parts under warranty policy, or GST adjustments. DealerPay reconciles every payment against submitted invoices and flags discrepancies immediately. We investigate via the portal's deduction details and file a correction claim or raise a dispute on your behalf.
